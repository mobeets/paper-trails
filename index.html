Reading scientific papers sounds hard, but it's easy if you start small.

Each week, I'll pick a simple topic—think "fire," "dirt," or "orca whales"—and read a high-impact scientific paper related to that topic. I'll send out a short email once a week about some of the things I learned along the way. My goal won't be to summarize the paper, necessarily, but to highlight how seemingly everyday topics end up informing larger scientific questions. For example, week #1 is about fire, which ends up being intimately related to both climate change and satellite imagery.

My hope is that these weekly emails will be a way to quickly learn something new about a broad range of scientific research. I would love it if you would read along with me! If you have any topic suggestions, let me know.

* Warning: I am not the woman in the bear costume. That is Susan Sontag.
